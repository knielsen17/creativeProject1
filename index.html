Statistics Distributions

There are many distributions that statisticians use to model many things in the natural world. There are more than I can list here, but through this website, I hope to give information on at least all the distributions listed here. And I will hopefully provide enough background to make the information useful. The first thing you need to understand is that all distributions are split into two categories. Discrete distributions are distributions that are based on discrete data meaning that the data can only take certain values. Continuous distributions are just the opposite. The probability mass function is the probability at different k. The probability mass function is only for discrete distributions. The probability denstity function is for continuous distributions. And they are the probability denstity at a given point in the support. There are going to be means and variances for all of these distributions.
